Manhattan Trails

Find green spaces tucked between skyscrapers and the Hudson River.

Inwood Hill Park

Inwood Hill Park Loop

Manhattan's last remaining old-growth forest. Walk through ancient caves and take in views of the Hudson River and the Palisades.

📍 Inwood 📏 2.5 miles Easy
Fort Tryon Park

Fort Tryon Park & The Heather Garden

Historic hilltop park featuring the largest public garden in the NYC parks system. Winding paths offer stunning Hudson River views and a peek at The Met Cloisters.

📍 Washington Heights 📏 1.8 miles Easy
Riverside Park

Riverside Park Waterfront Trail

A scenic 4-mile stretch along the Hudson River, passing memorials, playgrounds, and the 79th Street Boat Basin.

📍 Upper West Side 📏 4.0 miles Easy

Brooklyn Trails

Explore coastal marshes, woodland paths, and waterfront greenways in Kings County.

Prospect Park

Prospect Park Forest Loop

Brooklyn's beloved park features the borough's only remaining forest. The winding loop takes you through meadows, past a lake, and through dense woodland.

📍 Park Slope 📏 3.3 miles Easy
Marine Park

Marine Park Salt Marsh Trail

NYC's largest park boasts one of the most pristine salt marshes in the northeast. This flat, peaceful trail is a birdwatcher's paradise.

📍 Marine Park 📏 2.0 miles Easy
Greenwood Cemetery

Green-Wood Cemetery Walking Trail

Rolling hills, Victorian monuments, and a population of feral monk parakeets make this one of Brooklyn's most unique walking sites.

📍 Sunset Park 📏 3.0 miles Easy

Queens Trails

From oceanside boardwalks to forest preserves, Queens has nature for every New Yorker.

Jamaica Bay

Jamaica Bay Wildlife Refuge Loop

Run by the National Park Service, this refuge offers two trail loops around a brackish bay. Witness thousands of migratory birds.

📍 Broad Channel 📏 3.5 miles Easy
Alley Pond Park

Alley Pond Park Nature Trail

Home to NYC's oldest living inhabitant — a 350-year-old tulip tree — Alley Pond Park offers trails through freshwater wetlands.

📍 Oakland Gardens 📏 2.2 miles Easy
Forest Park

Forest Park Ridge Trail

Sitting atop a glacial moraine, Forest Park's ridge trails wind through one of the largest oak forests in NYC.

📍 Woodhaven 📏 4.1 miles Moderate

The Bronx Trails

The Bronx offers rugged forest trails, serene rivers, and dramatic escarpments.

Pelham Bay Park

Hunter Island Loop

NYC's largest park includes a loop through a rocky, forested peninsula featuring rocky shorelines and lagoon views.

📍 Pelham Bay 📏 2.8 miles Moderate
Van Cortlandt Park

Northwest Forest Trail

Ancient oaks canopy the path, and the John Muir Trail section cross through Van Cortlandt Park in the Bronx.

📍 Riverdale 📏 3.6 miles Moderate
Bronx River Greenway

Bronx River Greenway Walk

Running through the Bronx Zoo and NY Botanical Garden, the path offers an eclectic mix of wildlife and riverside nature.

📍 Bronx Zoo area 📏 5.0 miles Easy

Staten Island Trails

The Greenbelt offers NYC's most rugged and expansive hiking, just a ferry ride away.

The Greenbelt

Greenbelt High Rock Trail

High Rock Park's trails wind through swamps, ponds, and forests with challenging terrain and significant biodiversity.

📍 New Dorp 📏 4.5 miles Moderate
Conference House Park

Coastal Trail

At the southernmost tip of NYC, this trail hugs the shoreline of Raritan Bay before ducking into forest.

📍 Tottenville 📏 3.2 miles Easy
Snug Harbor

Cultural Center Trail

Wander through the New York Chinese Scholar's Garden, wetland boardwalks, and meadow restoration areas.

📍 Livingston 📏 1.5 miles Easy
